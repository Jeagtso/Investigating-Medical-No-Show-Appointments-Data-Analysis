Introduction¶

his dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row

  • ScheduledDay tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment.
  • Neighborhood indicates the location of the hospital.
  • Scholarship indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia.
  • The No-Show column: it says No if the patient showed up to their appointment, and Yes if they did not show up.

Research Questions¶

  • ### What is the overall precentage of no-show appointments?
  • ### Is there a corelation between gender and no-show appointments?
  • ### Does Receiving SMS have an effect on showing up?
  • ### Does the Neighbourhood effect showing up to the appointment?
  • ### General search on numirc features that might have an effect on showing up.
  • ### Does frequently Scheduled patients tend to show up more?
  • ### Does the day of the week effects no-show appointments?
  • ### Does the waiting days effects no-show appointments?

Conclusions¶

  • The data initially had 110527 and after cleaning up it only had 106177 record which means around around 4% of the data was bad Data.
  • 20% of all Appointments are no-show appointments
  • There are 69595 Females and 36582 Males Which means Females are around 52% more than males
  • There is no relation between gender and no appointments since around 25% for both Males and Females are no show appointments
  • Receiving SMS seems to have a negative impact, 44.62 % of all patients who received an SMS are no-show appointments, maybe there's a problem with the SMS wording, This requires more investigation
  • Most Patients who show up for their appointments are from JARDIM CAMBURI
  • Having a Scholarship doesn't have any effect on no-show appointments, nor does Hipertension, Diabetes, Alcoholism, and Handcap. Although middle aged patients (50 and above) tend to show up more than most other ages
  • Frequent patients tend to show up for their appointments more than new patients
  • The Appointment day of the week doesn't have any effect on no-show appointments, but the workload on Saturday is way lower than the rest of the week.
  • The average waiting time from the scheduled day to appointment day is around 10 days, while the average waiting for show ups is 8 days while average waiting days for no-show is 15 days
  • Most appointment dates that are on the same day as the scheduled day tend to show up for their appointments

Limitations¶

There is few problems with the dataset given, like most ages are around 0 to 1 years which doesn't make much sense, and there's a lot of missing important features like the employment of the patient, the current data can only show the distribution and current trends, Maybe more mathimatical tests are required to get better results¶
